Immigrations to Malaysia (2020 to 2024)

Immigration, the very thing that birthed the diversity of our nation over a century ago. Over the years, many have come to our country to begin new journeys, each from diverse backgrounds and cultures. Statistical aspects highlight this journey, showing a constant increase in percentage of immigrant arrivals since 2020.

In this series of visualisations, we will explore various statistical aspects of immigration into our beloved country.

We begin with the map on the right, illustrating the percentage of immigrant arrivals in each state since 2020.

Most immigrants are driven by economic factors, particularly the availability of jobs, which makes urban areas more appealing to migrants.

Johor's prime appeal lies in its proximity to Singapore, coupled with a myriad of job opportunities, making it an attractive destination for immigrants.

East Malaysia is also a popular destination, known for its tranquil and affordable lifestyle, a little further from the fast-paced city life.

Take a moment to observe the percentages in the tooltips on the map.

As you can see, although the number of immigrants may appear large, for most states, those who have immigrated in the past four years comprise merely less than 1% of each state's population!

Now we move on to the next question: Where in the world do our newcomers originate?

The answer is everywhere! Let us move on to the world map below to see exactly which countries they come from.

Many from American countries choose Malaysia as a retirement destination due to its high yet affordable living standards, which include excellent healthcare and high-quality education.

Meanwhile, many Southeast Asians migrate for work, primarily in industries such as hospitality, security, manufacturing, and agriculture.

Now, let us explore one of the most intriguing aspects of immigration, a trend not just in Malaysia but across the globeâ€”the gender gap.

In 2021, the International Organization for Migration revealed that there were 29 million more male migrants than female migrants globally!

On the right, this graph displays the total number of arrivals in each state, categorised by gender, highlighting noticeable gaps between male and female arrivals.

We can observe that in nearly every state, the number of male immigrants exceeds that of female immigrants.

What accounts for this significant gap? Traditional mindsets still deeply rooted in society, though slowly improving, play a key role.

Women are often associated with household and caregiving roles, and migration disrupts these expectations, leading to disapproval in some cultures.

Now that we understand immigration demographics, let's examine its impact on a crucial economic component: inflation.

Arrivals saw a significant decline in 2020 due to the rise of Covid-19 and the implementation of lockdown restrictions.
